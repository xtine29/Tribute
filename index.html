Amelia Earhart

"Lady Lindy"

Biography

Amelia Mary Earhart was born on July 24, 1897 in Atchison, Kansas. On January 3, 1921 she took her first flying lesson and after 6 months, she had saved enough money to buy her first plane and named it "The Canary". Earhart was one of the world's most celebrated aviators and was the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.
Above all, Earhart was determined and ready to be known as the first woman to fly around the world. yet tragedy struck Earhart on one of her flight and had disappeared on July 2, 1937. After 17 days, the attempt to locate her remains were reluctantly called off by the government. A year after the incident, a Light House was built in her memory on the island she was supposed to go.

First woman to fly across the Atlantic, 20hrs 40min

Set speed record for of 181.18mph over a 3K course

First woman to fly solo nonstop coast to coast; set women’s nonstop transcontinental speed record, flying 2,447.8 miles in 19hrs 5min

First person to solo the 2,408-mile distance across the Pacific between Honolulu and Oakland, California

First person to fly solo nonstop from Mexico City to Newark, 14hrs 19min

Fall 1929

First woman to fly solo across the Atlantic, 14 hrs 56 min

Fall 1932
Elected president of the Ninety Nines, a new women’s aviation club which she helped to form

First person to fly solo from Los Angeles to Mexico City; 13hrs 23min

June 1, 1937
Began flight around the world June 1937; first person to fly from the Red Sea to India

Career

Earhart attended Ogontz, a girl’s finishing school in the suburbs of Philadelphia. She left in the middle of her second year to work as a nurse’s aide in a military hospital in Canada during WWI.

Earhart took a variety of jobs such as a photographer, truck driver, and stenographer to save money for her flying lessons and to buy her first plane.

Earhart being the first women to fly across the Atlantic and was known for her record-setting flights.
